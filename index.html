01 · About

A scientist learning to speak in models.

From the micro-world of pharmaceutical nanotechnology to the vast universe of biological sciences, I'm embarking on a thrilling quest into data science.

My work sits in the gap between hard science and software. I trained as a pharmaceutical scientist — formulation, drug delivery, regulatory frameworks — and I'm now translating that domain knowledge into machine learning systems for healthcare. The goal is the same as it ever was: safer therapies, faster.

I care about reproducibility, interpretability, and the unglamorous plumbing that makes scientific software actually usable. I write Python, train neural networks, wrangle messy regulatory corpora, and occasionally publish what I learn.
